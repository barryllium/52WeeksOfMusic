When my wife and I were looking to buy our first home, we looked at many houses. And going into each open house, each viewing, we would get excited and think "maybe this is going to be the one". After many houses were viewed and many hopes were dashed, we finally decided that we were going to hate the next house we visited. We ended up buying that house. The key, I guess, was telling ourselves that we would hate it in advance.

* *Here Come the Warm Jets* (1974)
* *Taking Tiger Mountain (By Strategy)* (1974)
* *Another Green World* (1975)
* *Before and After Science* (1977)
* *Ambient 1: Music for Airports* (1978)
* "An Ending (Ascent)" (1983)

This is not the first time I've happened upon Brian Eno during this journey. He came up a lot while listening to David Bowie, and the same goes for the Talking Heads. The problem is that he was my least favorite part of both of those weeks. So starting this week, I was certain I was going to hate everything.

Imagine my surprise when, starting with the very first song, I *didn't* hate everything. Sure, I didn't love everything about it, but there were things I actually liked! As a whole, *Here Come the Warm Jets* wasn't groundbreaking, and there wasn't a single song on it that I fell in love with, but there were a number of songs that had a good beat, or some nice keyboards, or some other element that I was not expecting. I was expecting all synth, all the time - and instead I got songs like "On Some Faraway Beach", with its laid-back keyboard/drums/background vocals arrangement. Sure, there are also disasters on the album like "Dead Finks Don't Talk", but there were also moments in almost every song that I could appreciate.

*Taking Tiger Mountain (By Strategy)* was more of the same - some songs that were just not my thing ("Back in Judy's Jungle", "Put a Straw Under Baby") for one reason or another, and some that I kind of liked. "Mother Whale Eyeless" had a really good beat and solid transitions (I really liked it whenever the drums and/or keyboards would kick in). "Third Uncle" bounced off of me the first time I listened to it, but the second time through the bass, guitars, and drums all stuck out more and pulled me in. And the album ends really well with "Taking Tiger Mountain", a calming song where the ambient sounds worked well with the keyboards and guitars to create a calming atmosphere. And it was one of my favorite vocal performances of the week - the vocals and chorus complemented the music really well.

Things continued looking up with *Another Green World*, just not at first. I *hated* the "saw" sound in "Sky Saw", which was a shame because I really liked the rest of the music. About every other song really worked for me after that, though. "St. Elmo's Fire" pairs the vocals and music together really well, and the harmonies were spot on. "The Big Ship" lives up to its name, as the music keeps building up and gives the song a big feel; the guitars and synth work really well in it also. The latter half of the album featured more ambient music, and those were the ones I liked most in the second half (as opposed to the few songs with lyrics). "Becalmed" really stood out for me; the moment when the orchestra comes in it really got my attention both times I listened to it.

And... that's where things peaked. Things didn't get terrible, but they just never got back to that point after that. *Before and After Science* felt like a let-down after *Another Green World*, with only "Here He Comes" grabbing me in the slightest - it had a really good feel with its piano/percussion/guitar arrangement. But the rest of the songs on this album, and then on *Ambient 1: Music for Airports* and "An Ending (Ascent)" ended up mostly being in one of two categories:

* Ambient music: I *liked* most of this, but it's just never what I'm going to choose unless I'm meditating or something.
* Experiments that didn't work for me: this is "Kurt's Rejoinder" and the weird sounds happening the entire time, or "Energy Fools the Magician" where the cymbals are too much, too often.

More than anything, I'm glad I didn't hate everything this week. The last few months, I've had a handful of weeks that were just hard to get through, because I had albums that I just downright hated. But credit where it's due, most of this week was pleasant. It's nothing I'll ever revisit - but even things like *Ambient 1: Music for Airports* were nice to listen to. And all I had to do was tell myself I was going to hate all of it.

* Favorite Album: Easily *Another Green World*. Over half of the songs I marked as standing out in a good way came from this album. And it felt like a bridge for his career as well, containing both his art-rock and ambient music work. This album was clearly stronger from beginning to end than any of the others.
* Favorite Song: A two-horse race between "Taking Tiger Mountain" and "St. Elmo's Fire", with "St. Elmo's Fire" getting the nod because of how well the harmonies worked for me.
