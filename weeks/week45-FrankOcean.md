🎶 Get out of my dreams, get into my car 🎵 ... oh, wait, not that Ocean...

* “Novacane” (2011)
* “Swim Good” (2011)
* *channel ORANGE* (2012)
* *Blonde* (2016)
* “Chanel” (2017)

The first few times I heard anyone mention Frank Ocean, I 100% thought they were talking about [Billy Ocean](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zNgcYGgtf8M). Once I realized they weren't the same person, I decided to just keep deciding that they were. But after this week, I'm going to let that schtick go - because I legit enjoyed listening to Frank Ocean, and have now heard many more songs by him than the one Billy Ocean song that I know.

I had no idea what to expect - all I really knew was the name; I didn't know what genre(s) he covered or even what kind of music to expect at all. From the initial singles from 2011 all the way through "Chanel" in 2017, though, I found myself liking the feel and catchy beat of almost every song (I'm just going to ignore the tracks that aren't really songs in *channel ORANGE* and *Blonde*). I could feel the seeds of what he was doing in "Novacane" and "Swim Good" - but he really hits a stride in *channel ORANGE*.

The first song I *really* got into was "Sweet Life". Right before the 1-minute mark, the keyboards come in and the music picks up, and it fully gets my attention at that point. The subtle horns, the bass, and the background vocals carried the song along through some good transitions, and by that point I felt comfortable that this was going to be a good week.

Later, *channel ORANGE* hits a really good two-song pairing with "Pyramids" and "Lost". "Pyramids" absolutely crushes its first five minutes - I am 100% there for all of it: the musical build-up, the vocals, the sick beat that starts around the 1.5-minute mark. And then... the second half of the song happens. It's not even like the second half of the song is bad; it's that the first half is so damn good that it feels like a letdown (though my second listen made me appreciate it a little bit more). Luckily, it gets followed up by "Lost", a song with a really good feel and flow to it, transitioning really well between the verses and chorus. The bass and percussion sound great together, and as the song goes on, it keeps captivating me more.

Later on in the album, "Bad Religion" and "Pink Matter" bring another great back-to-back set. I loved the simple vocals and organ arrangement to start "Bad Religion", and that gets even better when the piano joins in; only to get even better when the cello/other strings come in and the vocals pop more. The whole song has a very powerful feel to it. "Pink Matter" follows it up with more great strings (and the vocals match up with them well). It's an emotional performance with André 3000 coming in and killing it vocally and on the guitar.

With *channel ORANGE* landing so well for me, I was primed to love "Blonde". And somehow I both really liked it *and* felt a bit let down by it, because it didn't quite deliver the highs the way *channel ORANGE* did for me. But it's not without some serious highlights. "Pink + White" starts out great with the piano/bass/drums, and then the guitars and strings come in halfway through and elevate the song even higher. Even when the music is at its "busiest", I still love the flow and feel.

The middle of the album is full of "almosts" for me. I can't say enough how close "Solo" and "Self Control" just miss the mark for me, and frustratingly so. The keyboards, the verses, and the flow are all great in "Solo", but the chorus just feels disjointed and pushes me away - a bummer because the song has such a strong presence. "Self Control" is even more frustrating, because I *really* like how effective the guitar and vocals are, and how fantastic the orchestra is in the background. If it weren't for a weird intro and a strange sound on top of the orchestra that I didn't like, this is a song that would be in the running for favorite song of the week.

The best part of the album for me, though, comes in a stretch near the end: "White Ferrari", "Siegfried", and "Godspeed". All three songs felt more personal, more intimate. The vulnerability in each track made me pay even more attention, and the keyboards/organ in each complemented the vocals and set a great mood. Each song has some minor things (spoken word, disconnected synth, programmed vocals) - but each song has so much to it that it doesn't really matter. And, for possibly the first time ever, a second listen even made me appreciate the spoken word part in the final third of "White Ferrari" (before now, my rule was that spoken word only works in the middle of a Boyz II Men song - you know, where the guy with the deep voice says "Baby... I'm sorry I didn't mow the lawn... and I shouldn't have lied to you about it... please take me back baby, PLEASE!"). Turns out good lyrics and good music can make you appreciate a song no matter what.

And to end the week, "Chanel" left me wanting more. There are a few weird vocal moments early, but after that the vocals are great, with some nice layered vocals and harmonies. The piano and percussion sound great together, and the entire composition is engaging. I've learned that there are a lot of questions about whether he'll ever make another album - but this left me wanting more.

And that's the best way I can sum up this week - I would totally be up for some more Frank Ocean. The sample size is small, but I liked almost everything I heard, and since I didn't know what to expect at all this week, it was an awesomely pleasant surprise.

* Favorite Album: *channel ORANGE* gets this, because it was so consistent and had such high highs. Even outside of the pairs of songs I mentioned earlier, everything else hit on some level almost without fail. For as much as I also liked "Blonde", there was just something about *channel ORANGE* that was a step above for me.
* Favorite Song: There are a few songs here that are in the running. "Pink + White" and "Bad Religion" are really high up there for me, but the first half of "Pyramids" is so damn good. From the 1.5-minute mark to the halfway point, this was one of my favorite beats of the last year, let alone this week. So my favorite half song wins favorite song this week.

Next week will be a different kind of experiment for me. For years I've heard people talk about how great this band is - in fact, it was a former co-worker's favorite band. But I really only know one song by this band, and *I don't like it*. So let's see how next week treats me when I lean into Rush...
